Who can Give Blood?

Any healthy adult, both male and female, can donate blood once in every three months. Good health of the donor must be fully ensured.

Criteria to Donate Blood


Most people can give blood. You can give blood if you:

(i) are fit and healthy.
(ii) weigh between 7 stone 12 lbs and 25 stone, or 50kg and 158kg.
(iii) are aged between 17 and 66 (or 70 if you have given blood before)
(iv) are over 70 and have given a full blood donation in the last two years.

Who should NOT Donate Blood?


The following categories of people should avoid giving blood:

(i) Pregnant or lactating women, or those who have recently had an abortion.
(ii) Persons who are on steroids, hormonal supplements or certain specified medication.
(iii) Persons with multiple sexual partners or those who are addicted to drugs.
(iv) Persons who have had an attack of infection like jaundice, rubella, typhoid or malaria.
(v) Persons who have undergone surgery in the previous six months.
(vi) Persons who have consumed alcohol in the 12 hours prior to donation.
